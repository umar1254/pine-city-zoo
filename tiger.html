TIGER

Size and Weight: Tigers are the largest cats on Earth, with adults weighing up to 720 pounds (363 kg) and measuring up to 6 feet (2 meters) in length. The Siberian tiger is the biggest subspecies.


Unique Stripes: Each tiger has a unique pattern of stripes, much like human fingerprints. These stripes help them blend into their environment, providing camouflage while hunting.


Hunting Behavior: Tigers are solitary hunters, typically stalking their prey at night. They rely on stealth and strength, often dragging their prey into cover to eat.
